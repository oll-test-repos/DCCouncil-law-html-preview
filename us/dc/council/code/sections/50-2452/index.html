§ 50–2452. Vehicles eligible for immobilization or impoundment.

*NOTE: This section includes amendments by emergency legislation that will expire on February 22, 2026. To view the text of this section after the expiration of all emergency and temporary legislation, click this link: Permanent Version.*

(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the following vehicles shall be deemed immobilization-eligible vehicles and subject to immobilization or impoundment as described in § 50-2453:

(1) Unidentifiable vehicles;

(2) Vehicles parked on a public right-of-way or public space in the District, or on private property under the jurisdiction or management of the District, against which:

(A) There have been issued 2 or more warrants;

(B) The Mayor has assessed 10 or more vehicle infraction points pursuant to § 50-2451; or

(C) There are 2 or more unpaid notices of infraction that the owner was deemed to have admitted or that were sustained after a hearing pursuant to § 50-2209.02; and

[(3)] Abandoned or dangerous vehicles.

(May 2, 2025, D.C. Law 25-325, § 122, 72 DCR 1284; Nov. 24, 2025, D.C. Act 26-210, § 6072(a), 0 DCR 0.)

Applicability

Section 6072(b) of D.C. Act 26-210 repealed the applicability provision of section 121 of D.C. Law 25-325 that impacted this section. Therefore the amendment to this section by §122(a)(2)(B) of Law 25-325 has been implemented.

Section 6072(b) of D.C. Act 26-146 repealed the applicability provision of section 121 of D.C. Law 25-325 that impacted this section. Therefore the amendment to this section by §122(a)(2)(B) of Law 25-325 has been implemented.

Applicability of D.C. Law 25-325: § 401 of D.C. Law 25-325 provided that the creation of subparagraph (a)(2)(B) in this section by § 122 of D.C. Law 25-325 is subject to the inclusion of the law’s fiscal effect in an approved budget and financial plan. Therefore that amendment has not been implemented.
