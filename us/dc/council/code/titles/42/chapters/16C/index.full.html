Code of the District of Columbia

Chapter 16C. Tangled Title Disclosure.

§ 42–1681. Definitions.

For purposes of this chapter, the term:

(1) "Department" means the Department of Housing and Community Development.

(2) "Funeral services establishment" shall have the same meaning as set forth in § 3-402(11).

(3) "Heir" shall have the same meaning as set forth in § 20-101(c).

(4) "Legatee" shall have the same meaning as set forth in § 20-101(g).

(5) "Survivor of the deceased" means any individual with whom funeral services are being arranged.

(Oct. 1, 2025, D.C. Law 26-41, § 101, 72 DCR 8881.)

§ 42–1682. Tangled title disclosure.

(a) No later than [March 30, 2026], the Department shall, in consultation with the Superior Court of the District of Columbia and legal service providers, create and make publicly available a tangled title information sheet detailing the steps necessary, after the death of a real property owner, to legally transfer the real property to a decedent's heirs or legatees. The information sheet also shall include information on legal service providers who offer low-cost or no-cost representation in estate administration cases.

(b)(1) No later than 5 days after the final disposition of the deceased, or at the time of providing to survivors of the deceased any death certificate or death certificates ordered by the funeral services establishment from the Department of Health, whichever occurs first, a funeral service establishment shall provide the tangled title information sheet made available by the Department to survivors of the deceased.

(2) A funeral service establishment that fails to provide a tangled title information sheet pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection may be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed $100 for each offense.

(Oct. 1, 2025, D.C. Law 26-41, § 102, 72 DCR 8881.)
